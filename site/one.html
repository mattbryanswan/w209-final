What About the Violations?

What's in an A?

The Back Story

To ensure high standards of cleanliness and safety at New York's various dining establishments, the Department of Health and Mental Hygiene conducts random inspections and records violations observed during the inspection. Depending on the severity and number of violations observed, the restaurant is given a grade of A, B, or C.

Grades on Public Display

Once a grade is assigned, a card noting that grade is given to the restaurant for display. There are strict rules as to where that card must be displayed, ensuring it can be viewed by a potential patron before - or within a few steps of - entering a dining establishment.

A or Bust

It would appear this system both motivates the restaurant owners to maintain a high standard of service and puts diners' minds as ease by being completely transparent about the results. This certainly seems supported by the fact that a majority of restaurants are indeed receiving A grades. But it feels like there's something we're leaving out...

"A" Simple Majority
