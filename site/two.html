Let's Find Out!

A's have violations, two!

Violations aren't listed on the card

Even though a restaurant has to display their grade, the card they are given does not include the individual violations received. This creates a scenario in which many may assume that Grade A is an equivalent for having no violations. This is, however, not reflective of reality.

Grade A restaurants have, on average, two violations

It's true! While restaurants receiving grades of B or C have more violations - and more egregious ones - restaurants receiving an A grade do not have clean sheets either.

What's lurking in Grade A restaurants?

This forces us to ask the question - if Grade A restaurants have, on average, two violations... what are these violations for? If they're for simple things, maybe we can forgive and forget. For some A graded restaurants, it may not be that simple.
